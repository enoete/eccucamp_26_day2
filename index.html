Module 1 of 5
IMAGE
GENERATION

What it does

  • Generates still images from text prompts
  • 100+ models — GPT Image, Nano Banana Pro, Flux, Stable Diffusion
  • Image-to-image: upload a reference, generate variations
  • Inpainting: edit specific parts of an image
  • Upscaling: increase resolution for print/screen quality
  • Style transfer: apply a visual style to any image

Why it matters for your film

  • Every concept image in your library comes from here
  • Your moodboard will be rebuilt with your own generated assets
  • Your storyboard panels become visual references, not sketches
  • Week 3: these images become the starting frames for your video clips
⭐
Which clients use this most
All four — but Outsource Dev and Genzcarwash will lean heaviest on image generation for character-driven and location scenes. IRD will use it to generate infographic backgrounds and animation frames.
Module 2 of 5
CHARACTER
BUILDER

What it does

  • Locks a character's face, age, style, and visual identity
  • Saves the character as a reusable reference asset
  • Every generation using that reference = same person, different scene
  • Supports up to 80 saved characters on the Infinite plan
  • Works across image generation AND video generation
  • Can lock multiple characters for multi-person scenes

Why it matters for your film

  • Without this: your character looks different in every clip
  • With this: same face, same style, scene after scene
  • This is what makes AI film feel like a real film
  • Build your character today — it follows you into Week 3 production
🔴
Red Alert
Build your character before you generate a single video clip. Trying to add character consistency after the fact is extremely painful. Do it now, in Week 1, while you still have time.
Module 3 of 5
VIDEO
GENERATION

What it does

  • Text-to-video: describe a scene, get a clip
  • Image-to-video: animate a still image — most consistent results
  • Start frame / end frame: lock the first and last image, AI fills the motion between
  • Lip sync: upload audio, AI syncs character lip movement to voice
  • Models: Kling 3.0, Veo 3.1, Sora 2, Seedance 2.0 — all accessible
  • Clips typically 5–10 seconds — assembled in CapCut

Why it matters for your film

  • This is how your film gets made in Week 3
  • Image-to-video from your concept library = your scenes
  • Start/end frame chaining = visual continuity between clips
  • Lip sync from your ElevenLabs narration = talking characters
  • You will use this every day in Week 3
You won't touch Video Generation until Day 9 — but understanding it now changes how you build your Week 1 assets.
Module 4 of 5
DIRECTOR
MODE

What it does

  • Conversational film-making — describe your film in plain language
  • Director builds a complete multi-scene sequence from your description
  • Maintains character consistency across all generated scenes
  • Integrates music, voice, visuals, and SFX in one workflow
  • Up to ~5 minutes of continuous film per session
  • Iterate by talking: "make the market scene warmer" "hold the face longer"

Why it matters for your film

  • This is your primary production tool in Week 3
  • You bring: storyboard + character + audio + creative direction
  • Director builds the film around what you've made in Weeks 1 & 2
  • The better your Week 1 prep — the better your Director output
  • You get a full preview of Director mode on Day 9
⭐
Gold Star
Director mode is the most powerful tool in this camp. Everything you're doing in Weeks 1 and 2 is preparation for the moment you open Director mode and describe your film in conversation.
Module 5 of 5
AUDIO
GENERATION

What it does

  • Music generation: text-to-music powered by Google Lyria 3
  • Describe mood, genre, instruments — get a full original track
  • Voiceover: text-to-speech in 30+ languages with emotional control
  • SFX generation: describe a sound effect, get the audio file
  • All audio usable directly inside Director mode
  • Commercial rights included on Infinite plan

Why it matters — and the distinction

  • You have Suno AI for dedicated music education — that's Week 2
  • OpenArt audio is the scoring layer — quick music to match a generated scene
  • When you're in Director mode and need a track fast — OpenArt audio delivers
  • ElevenLabs remains the narration tool — more dialect control
  • OpenArt audio = speed. Suno/ElevenLabs = craft.

The Client Meeting
THREE ROLES.
EVERY MEETING.

The Presenter

  • Introduces the team
  • Walks through creative direction
  • Shows the moodboard
  • Confident, concise, no rambling

The Note-Taker

  • Writes everything the client says
  • Not just answers — all of it
  • Clients reveal what they didn't know you needed
  • This person is critical

The Questioner

  • Asks the prepared questions
  • Asks follow-ups from what the client reveals
  • Listens fully before the next question
  • Closes: "What haven't we asked?"
The Golden Rule
Present enough to show you've done the work.
Then stop talking and listen.
The client's words in this meeting are worth more than anything you will produce in the next three weeks. Your job in that room is to learn — not to impress.
⭐
Gold Star — Career Advice
Always end a client meeting with: "What haven't we asked that we should have?" The answer to that question is always the most important thing you'll hear.
The Meeting Structure
WHAT YOU
PRESENT
1. Team introduction — who you are, your role in the studio
2. One sentence: "Here's how we're thinking about your film"
3. The moodboard — show it, explain three decisions, then ask: "Does this feel like your brand?"
4. Your question list — ask each, listen fully before the next
5. Close with: "What haven't we asked that we should have?"
Final Task

Assign Roles & Rehearse

⏱ 20 MINUTES · TEAM PREP
  • Assign: Presenter · Note-Taker · Questioner
  • Review and finalise your question list — is it rich enough?
  • Presenter says the one-sentence creative direction out loud — right now
  • 3-minute internal rehearsal — Presenter walks through the meeting structure
  • Add "What haven't we asked?" to the bottom of every question list
